Bakery

Cannoli $3.99

Cannoli are one of the best known and loved desserts in the Sicilian tradition. Our cannoli's are ricotta-filled tube-shaped shells of pastry dough made of flour, cocoa, coffee, sugar and butter. The filling is made of ricotta and confectioner's sugar.

Panettone $4.99

Panettone is a sweet bread originating from Milan. They are made and enjoyed during Christmas, but we make them year round!

Cornetto $2.99

A cornetto, meaning "little horn", is an Italian variation of the Austrian kipferl. It differs from a croissant in being softer and containing less butter. The main ingredients of a cornetto are pastry dough, eggs, butter, water and sugar.

Tiramisu $4.99

Tiramisu in Italian literally means “pick me up.” It makes sense – who wouldn’t feel uplifted by a delicious dessert made of coffee-coated soft cookies called Savoiardi (lad fingers), a delicious mascarpone cream and chocolate?

Drinks

Caffè $4.99

While caffè does literally translates to coffee, a caffè is also a shot of espresso. It is served in a tiny cup and drank all throughout the day. When ordering, you order un caffè and not un espresso. congue eu mauris nec, pretium tincidunt nulla.

Marocchino $6.50

Grazie mille to the genius in Alessandria that married cocoa and espresso together to invent the marocchino! It’s a shot of espresso, a layer of foam, and a sprinkle of cacao powder in a glass mug that has been dusted with cocoa powder.

Caffè Latte $5.99

Order just a latte in Italy and you might be surprised to be served a tall glass of milk. What we in the US call a latte is a caffè latte in Italy. It’s ⅓ espresso, 2/3 heated milk, and a little foam.

Shakerato $3.99

The Shakerato is Italy's answer to an American iced coffee. It is prepered with expresso and ice cubes. Its popular all over italy!
